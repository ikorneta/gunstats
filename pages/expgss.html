Attitude towards housing discrimination among white and black gun owners and non-owners from 2004 onwards

While looking at the data, I discovered that the question with discriminated the most between young white male gun owners and all others groups of the American society was the housing discrimination question. This question is phrased in the GSS as follows: Suppose there is a community-wide vote on the general housing issue. There are two possible laws to vote on: a. One law says that a homeowner can decide for himself whom to sell his house to, even if he prefers not to sell to (negroes/blacks/African-Americans). b. The second law says that a homeowner cannot refuse to sell to someone because of their race or color. Which law would you vote for? The possible answers are Owner Decides, Cant Discriminate, Neither and Dont Know.

This question was initially very divisive throughout the American society - in 1980, attitudes ranged from just over 20% to over 80% in favour of the Cant Discriminate answer in the various age/race/sex/gun-ownership segments of the society. However, in the early 2000s, opinions coalesced in favour of the Cant Discriminate answer, to the end result that in 2012, every single segment of the GSS respondents was over 55% in favour of this answer - every single segment but young white male gun owners. And for them, the answer was only 40.4% in favour of the anti-discriminatory reply.

I wanted to find out whether this particular result was statistically significant.
